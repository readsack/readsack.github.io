Title: Will To Power
Date: 22 March, 2026
---
First, let me clear some things, since Nietzche's definition of power, differs majorly from the current, traditional definition of power.

Power is not the control or influence over others. Instead, Nietzche argues that those who seek to control others, are actually the weak, who are trying to grasp at the false feeling of superiority by doing so.

The type of power Nietzche speaks about, is a type of power we embody and express within ourselves and it is independent of others. 

More Power means more **Life**, not by an expansion of it or an extension of it, but by the ascension of it. It means ascending to the higher level of capacity in one's being.

According to nietzche, the increasing one's power is equal to growth. It is the surpassing of your own level, the overcoming of your own limits.

Nietzche outlines a way for one to gain power. It is the setting of high, lofty, noble goals. These goals must meet certain requirements, for them to be power-increasing. Here are those requirements:

1. It must provide an appropriate challenge.

2. It must promote one's wellbeing.

3. It must be in line with your way of personal excellence.

4. It must be self chosen. Self-chosen here implies that you are the one choosing this goal of your own volition, by your own values. It must not be something imprinted on you by your peers, family or society. It must be something unique and authentic to you.
____

One must not be concerned with the purpose of humanity or the purpose of life. The only thing which should concern one is the **purpose of one's own existence.**
If one cannot answer this question, then one must look to the setting of the kind of goals mentioned above, and somewhere along the way, depending on the person, one will find their answer to this question.

> **Resistances** should be welcome by one who's seeking **power**, since they allow him the opportunity to grow further beyond. By overcoming resistances arising one's path, one can increase his power further and go beyond his current self. When one sets out on the path of his goal, resistances are bound to arise, and it is in the process of overcoming this resistances, that the increase in one's power occurs. Hence, by continually overcoming the resistances as they arise in one's path, one can keep growing and increasing their power.

One should dedicate a time from each and every day in the attainment of their goal, without exception. 

After one has attained their goal, one should not pause or gloat about their achievement, rather set their sights to greater heights, and set an even higher goal for themselves. 

One who wills power, will not be satisfied with what they have created or attained. Their will to power, will soon make them grow dissatisfied with their creation and make the creation seem lacking.

> **The agent in pursuit of power doesn't seek achievement, he seeks achieving**
> *- Bernard Reginster*
