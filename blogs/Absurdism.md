Title: Absurdism

The famous philosophical truth, about this world having no inherent meaning, is pointless. Because it's not something we can do anything about, nor we should be bothering over it. 'The question about the point of the world itself is pointless'

>  **"We are creatures wired to search for meaning, living in a world which offers nothing but cold silence."**

But this curelty is only felt, when confronted with this truth. Camus calls this the glimpse of the Absurd. 

Once faced with the Absurd, one has three options. 

First option, one can commit suicide. Camus says that the people who voluntarily end their lives, do so in outbursts of emotion, other than cold, hard, reason. The point 'the world is meaningless' does not translate to 'there is no point in living'. 

Second option is what Camus calls philosophical suicide. This is basically anchoring your faith in some meaning giving delusion and living your life based on the rules and systems of values it provides. This comprises of the various religions, gods and other systems of make-believe, which propose the idea that life might have a meaning.

Third option, the one Camus recommends, is a revolt. A revolt against the meaninglessness of the world. One has to recognize, that even if the world has no meaning, he must keep living inspite of it. The life one lives, is self-justifying, self-fulfilling and does not need any higher meaning to be justified. 

> **"The struggle itself towards the heights is enough to fill a man's heart"** *- Albert Camus*

## Camus's Absurd Man
The absurd man, is the one who first glimpses the Absurd, and chooses the third option, as described above. His three main qualities include

**Living In Revolt:** The absurd man always lives in revolt against the Absurdity of the universe. This revolt is in the act of continuing to live, better and joyful, in spite of the meaninglessness of the world.

**Fundamental Freedom:** The absurd man is fundamentally free. He isn't bound by the constraints of ethics or traditional morality, nor is he weighed down by the past regrets or future concerns.

**Living With Passion:** The absurd man thrives on living more and more. It's not about his experiences being good or bad, rather, it's about the sheer quantity of his experiences and being completely in the moment while experiencing them.
